Build a Discord bot for my Writing Sprint app that works just like the Sprinto bot — familiar commands, same flow, but powered by my existing backend.
Tech stack:

discord.js v14, TypeScript
ws library for WebSocket connection to my server
node-fetch for REST API calls
Lives in artifacts/discord-bot/

Environment variables:

DISCORD_TOKEN
DISCORD_CLIENT_ID
API_BASE_URL — e.g. https://typing-race--yarnnapoljacopo.replit.app
WS_URL — e.g. wss://typing-race--yarnnapoljacopo.replit.app/ws


Commands — modelled on Sprinto:
/sprint — Start a default 15-minute sprint, beginning in 1 minute. Bot posts a countdown message then announces when the sprint starts, including the room code and a join link to the website.
/sprint for [duration] — Start a sprint of a custom length. Examples: /sprint for 30, /sprint for 1hr, /sprint for 45 mins
/sprint in [time] — Start a default 15-minute sprint delayed by a set time. Example: /sprint in 5 starts in 5 minutes.
/sprint for [duration] in [time] — Custom duration with a delayed start. Example: /sprint for 30 in 10 starts a 30-minute sprint in 10 minutes.
/sprint for [duration] at [:mm] — Schedule a sprint to start at a specific clock minute. Example: /sprint for 20 at :30 starts a 20-minute sprint at the next half hour.
/sprint mode [regular/open/goal] — Choose the sprint mode before starting. Regular = private writing, word count only. Open = everyone sees each other's text live. Goal = set a target word count. If mode is not specified, default to regular.
/sprint goal [words] — Start a goal-mode sprint with a specific word target. Example: /sprint goal 500 starts a sprint where everyone is racing to 500 words.
/sprint random — Spin the wheel! Starts a sprint of a random length between 10 and 25 minutes.
/join [starting word count] — Join the active sprint in this channel. Starting word count defaults to 0. Example: /join 1200 if you already have 1200 words written and want to track new words only.
/words [final word count] — Submit your final word count when the sprint ends. The bot calculates words written by subtracting your starting count. Example: /words 1642
/words +[words written] — Submit just the words you wrote during the sprint, without needing to remember your starting count. Example: /words +342
/leave — Leave the current sprint.
/cancel — Cancel the sprint before it starts (creator or Sprint MC role only).
/end — End the sprint early (creator or Sprint MC role only).
/time — Show how much time is left in the current sprint.
/status — Show current sprint status and who has joined. No word count updates during the sprint — only shown at the end.
/pb — Show your personal best sprint (most words written in a single sprint).
/stats [@user] — Show your own or another user's total words written, number of sprints, and average WPM.

Bot behaviour — matching Sprinto's flow:

When /sprint is called, bot posts: "A sprint is starting in 1 minute! Type /join to join. Room code: SPRINT-XXXX | Join on the website: [link]"
During the join window, bot updates the message as people join
When the sprint starts, bot announces: "The sprint has begun! You have 15 minutes. Go go go! 🖊️"
At 1 minute remaining, bot warns: "One minute left!"
When the sprint ends, bot posts: "Time's up! Please submit your final word count with /words [count]. You have 2 minutes to submit."
After everyone submits (or the 2-minute window closes), bot posts the final scoreboard only — rankings, word counts, and WPM. No mid-sprint updates at all.
Bot then congratulates the top writer and shows total group word count.

No live updates during the sprint. The only word count reveal is the final scoreboard after the sprint ends. This matches the Sprinto experience.
WebSocket connection:

Connect to the room's WebSocket as a silent spectator named "DiscordBot"
Only listen for the sprint_ended event to trigger the final scoreboard
Ignore all participant_update and room_state events during the sprint

Sprint MC role:

If a Discord role named "Sprint MC" exists on the server, members with that role can cancel, end, or take over any sprint
Server admins always have Sprint MC powers

Channel behaviour:

Only one sprint can run per channel at a time
Sprints in different channels are independent
Bot ignores sprint commands outside of allowed channels if /setup-channel has been configured

Do not modify any existing backend files. The bot is a standalone process that only uses the existing REST API and WebSocket.
